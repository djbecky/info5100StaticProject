Fun of Flags

(Inspired by Dr. Sheldon Cooper!)

Red and white are the most frequently used colors in flags worldwide, more than 70% countries have either of these two colors on their flags. And 7 colors can cover all the colors used across all the countries.

Most of the countries have <=4 colors on their flag, and 3 color is the most popular pattern seen;
Almost half of the countries have <= 2 shapes on their flags. Among these, more than half choose to use only 1 element on their flag.
So, the design of the flag is really simple! 3 colors and 1 element, you have a flag!

We can see that Red is shown in top position in every continent and every religion, this is consistent with our result in the first visualization, which tells that red is the most popular color.
Considering the second most popular color, we can divided the regions into two different kinds, Africa and Asia prefer green , while other continents prefer blue . This result is somewhat influenced by the religion practiced by the country. As Muslim prefers green and a large percentage of countries in Africa and Asia are Muslim, the color green appears more. In the same way, Catholic and other Christian religions prefer blue , thus continents where these two religions are concentrated (Oceania, S.America, N.America) have more of blue color in their flags.

Every religion not only has a preferred colored, they also have their own shape.
Stars and stripes are commonly used in every religion, crescent is preferred by Muslim for in their culture, crescent and stars are the symbols of Muslim country.
Crescent has long been a religious cult in Arabic area, for the day is too hot and people usually do all the work at night under the light of moon. Also in the history of Muslim countries, crescents and stars were one of the symbols of power and people still worship them. Similarly we see that crosses are preferred by the Catholic. According to history it is raised from Christian-civilization. For the death of Christ was on the cross for the happiness for his people and to remember his sacrifice, cross was adapted as a symbol of Christianity.
